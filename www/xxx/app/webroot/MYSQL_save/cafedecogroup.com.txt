1 eng German Beef Rouladen <p>Rouladen (or beef rolls) are typically filled with bacon, onion, mustard and pickles and are wrapped by a thin slice of beef. This delicious and traditional German recipe is generally for moments when the entire family is together, usually for Sunday lunch, and goes perfectly well with Potato Dumplings and red cabbage.</p>
<p><strong>Ingredients</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>6 slices of Beef – either ask the butchers to cut the beef ‘top round’ or buy a flank steak and do it yourself</li>
  <li>3 slices of lean bacon, chopped</li>
  <li>1 onion, sliced</li>
  <li>3 garlic dill pickles, sliced</li>
  <li>2 tbsp of butter</li>
  <li>Dijon or German style mustard </li>
  <li>Salt & pepper</li>
  <li>Corn starch</li>
  <li>1 - 2 cups water</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Method</strong></p>
<p>Start off by seasoning one side of the beef slices with salt and freshly ground pepper then spread a thin layer of Mustard over the top of the seasoning</p>
<p>Evenly distribute the bacon, pickle and onion slices on top of the mustard </p>
<p>Roll up the slices, tucking the ends in and securing the sides with skewers, wooden cocktail picks, or sew them together with thread to keep the ingredients from falling out</p>
<p>Heat butter in skillet and brown the Rouladen well on all sides. Be sure to not crowd Rouladen in the skillet or they will not brown nicely. If you do not have a large enough skillet, brown the Rouladen in small batches. Add any extra butter if need be</p>
<p>Once all of the rolls are well browned take them out of the skillet and add your 1 or 2 cups of hot water, gently stirring up browned bits</p>
<p>Return all of the Rouladen and any accumulated juices to skillet, bring to simmer and cover for around an hour and a half whilst leaving the heat on</p>
<p>Remove the Rouladen from the skillet and thicken the gravy with 1 or 2 tablespoons of Corn Starch mixed with a small amount of cold water. Stir the mixture and gravy together gently until it has slightly thickened</p>
<p>Season the gravy to your preferred taste with salt and freshly ground pepper. If you wish, add sour cream to the gravy</p>
<p>Remove the skewers, picks, or thread and serve the delicious Rouladen with the gravy

2 eng Koenigsberger Klopse <p>This classical German dish entails delightfully tender meatballs in a rich white wine-caper sauce. These delicious meatballs are traditionally served with potatoes and a vegetable side dish. This recipe serves four people.</p>
<p><strong>Ingredients</strong></p>
<p>Broth:</p>
<ul>
  <li>1 Small piece of bone marrow (optional)</li>
  <li>2 tsp of Vegetable broth</li>
  <li>4 1/2 cups of Water</li>
  <li>1 Small Onion, peeled and halved</li>
  <li>1 dried Bay leaf</li>
  <li>5 whole Black Peppercorns</li>
</ul>
<p>Meatballs:</p>
<ul>
  <li>2 small Onions, peeled and finely diced</li>
  <li>1 tbsp of Butter</li>
  <li>250g (1/2lb) ground Pork</li>
  <li>250g (1/2lb) ground Beef</li>
  <li>2 day old Buns, quickly wet them under running water and tear them up. If the bread is dripping then squeeze out the excess water</li>
  <li>1 Egg</li>
  <li>1/2 a bunch of Parsley, finely chopped </li>
  <li>1 tbsp of pickled Capers, lightly crushed</li>
  <li>1 tbsp of Anchovy paste or several pieces of Anchovy, finely chopped</li>
  <li>Salt and Pepper to taste</li>
</ul>
<p>Sauce:</p>
<ul>
  <li>2 tbsp of Butter</li>
  <li>2 tbsp of Flour, you may need more if you want your sauce thicker </li>
  <li>1/2 a Liter (500ml or 2-1/4 cups) of broth</li>
  <li>1 tbsp of whole Pickled Capers</li>
  <li>1 tbsp of fresh Lemon juice</li>
  <li>1/8 a Liter (125ml or 1/2 a cup) of Sour Cream</li>
  <li>2 tbsp of Whipping Cream</li>
  <li>175ml (3/4 of a cup of 6 fl. Oz) of dry White Wine or substitute the left over </li>
</ul>
<p>Broth</p>
<ul>
  <li>1 tsp of Sugar</li>
  <li>2 Egg Yolks, lightly beaten</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Method</strong></p>
<p>Broth:<p>
<p>Add all of the ingredients for the broth into a medium saucepan and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to medium and allow the broth to simmer for 40 minutes</p>

<p>Strain the contents from the broth and simmer again over low heat for another 20 minutes. Whilst waiting begin preparation on the meatballs</p>

<p>Meatballs:</p>
<p>Simmer the Onions and Butter over medium heat for a few minutes or until the Onions are translucent. Set them aside and allow them to cool</p>

<p>In a medium bowl add all of the ingredients for the Meatballs into a medium sized bowl and season with Salt and Pepper to your liking</p>

<p>Add the cooled onions to the bowl and with clean hands, mix everything together</p>

<p>Transfer the meat to a cutting board or clean work surface and roughly shape it into a 12” x 7” x 1” rectangle. Cut the mixture into 16 equal pieces and roll the pieces into balls</p>

<p>Transfer the broth to a skillet or a deep frying pan and gently place the meatballs in the broth to ensure that they are evenly cooked. Cover the pot and allow the meatballs to simmer gently for 15 minutes. If the meatballs are not simmered gently they will fall apart</p>

<p>Remove the meatballs from the broth and be sure to keep them warm whilst you make the white wine-caper sauce</p>

<p>Sauce:</p>
<p>Melt the butter in a medium saucepan over medium-high heat and stir in the flour and then add the broth</p>

<p>Add the remaining ingredients except for the egg yolks, season with salt and pepper to your taste, and cook cause over a low flame for 10 minutes</p>

<p>Remove the sauce from heat and mix in 2 tbsp of the sauce into the egg yolks and then stir into the sauce</p>

<p>Add the warm meatballs and cover the pot, allowing the mixture to stand for 10 minutes before serving</p> 2

  <li>1 Egg</li>
  <li>1/2 a bunch of Parsley, finely chopped </li>

  <li>1 tbsp of fresh Lemon juice</li>
  <li>1/8 a Liter (125ml or 1/2 a cup) of Sour Cream</li>
  <li>2 tbsp of Whipping Cream</li>
  <li>175ml (3/4 of a cup of 6 fl. Oz) of dry White Wine or substitute the left over </li>
</ul>
<p>Broth</p>

<p>Remove the sauce from heat and mix in 2 tbsp of the sauce into the egg yolks and then stir into the sauce</p>

1 eng About Us <p>Berliner German Bar &amp; Restaurant aims to bring Germany's well-known culinary tradition in a casual yet modern ambiance. <h3>Concept</h3>
<p>In early 2010, Cafe Deco Group saw a need to provide popular German favorites so the Group came up an idea to open a German restaurant in Hong Kong. After numerous meetings and discussions, we came up with "Berliner", which was derived from "Ich bin ein Berliner" or "I am a Berliner", a famous quote from a speech made by US President John F. Kennedy in June 26, 1963.</p>
<p>Berliner continued to expand quickly in convenient locations across Hong Kong. In July 2011, it opened in Olympian City 3, followed by Discovery Bay in October 2011, Wanchai in July 2012, Metroplaza in July 2014 and Tuen Mun Town Plaza in February 2015.</p>

1 JOIN 25 MySQL supports the following JOIN syntaxes for the table_references
part of SELECT statements and multiple-table DELETE and UPDATE
statements:

table_references:
    table_reference [, table_reference] ...

table_reference:
    table_factor
  | join_table

table_factor:
    tbl_name [[AS] alias] [index_hint)]
  | table_subquery [AS] alias
  | ( table_references )
  | { OJ table_reference LEFT OUTER JOIN table_reference
        ON conditional_expr }

join_table:
    table_reference [INNER | CROSS] JOIN table_factor [join_condition]
  | table_reference STRAIGHT_JOIN table_factor
  | table_reference STRAIGHT_JOIN table_factor ON conditional_expr
  | table_reference {LEFT|RIGHT} [OUTER] JOIN table_reference join_condition
  | table_reference NATURAL [{LEFT|RIGHT} [OUTER]] JOIN table_factor

join_condition:
    ON conditional_expr
  | USING (column_list)

index_hint:
    USE {INDEX|KEY} [FOR JOIN] (index_list)
  | IGNORE {INDEX|KEY} [FOR JOIN] (index_list)
  | FORCE {INDEX|KEY} [FOR JOIN] (index_list)

index_list:
    index_name [, index_name] ...

A table reference is also known as a join expression.

The syntax of table_factor is extended in comparison with the SQL
Standard. The latter accepts only table_reference, not a list of them
inside a pair of parentheses.

This is a conservative extension if we consider each comma in a list of
table_reference items as equivalent to an inner join. For example:

SELECT * FROM t1 LEFT JOIN (t2, t3, t4)
                 ON (t2.a=t1.a AND t3.b=t1.b AND t4.c=t1.c)

is equivalent to:

SELECT * FROM t1 LEFT JOIN (t2 CROSS JOIN t3 CROSS JOIN t4)
                 ON (t2.a=t1.a AND t3.b=t1.b AND t4.c=t1.c)

In MySQL, CROSS JOIN is a syntactic equivalent to INNER JOIN (they can
replace each other). In standard SQL, they are not equivalent. INNER
JOIN is used with an ON clause, CROSS JOIN is used otherwise.

In versions of MySQL prior to 5.0.1, parentheses in table_references
were just omitted and all join operations were grouped to the left. In
general, parentheses can be ignored in join expressions containing only
inner join operations. As of 5.0.1, nested joins are allowed (see
http://dev.mysql.com/doc/refman/5.0/en/nested-join-optimization.html).

Further changes in join processing were made in 5.0.12 to make MySQL
more compliant with standard SQL. These charges are described later in
this section.

Index hints can be specified to affect how the MySQL optimizer makes
use of indexes. For more information, see
http://dev.mysql.com/doc/refman/5.0/en/index-hints.html.
